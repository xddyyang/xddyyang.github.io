Revisualization of the architecture-urban history of Jiangnan in its Spatial context: A pilot comparative study in Twin Pagoda and Tongli

  • Visualized historical districts of Suzhou within a larger geographical context to understand the impact of geographical features and urbanization on the city's development.
  • Employed a combination of quantitative and qualitative methods: Conducted on-site inspections and interviews in Dual Tower District and Tongli Water Town; Documented key historical structures (e.g., ancient towers, bridges) through traditional measurements and hand-drawing; Integrated historical data and hand-drawn content to trace urban evolution; Digitized findings using AutoCAD and ArcGIS, creating a Suzhou historical architecture database.
  • Provided insights into Suzhou's urban history and development, highlighting the role of geography and historical structures.

Gentrification in Suzhou: A Tale of Urban Redevelopment

  • Investigated contemporary urban gentrification trends in second and third-tier Chinese cities, focusing on Suzhou. The aim was to identify potential factors contributing to middle-class growth and assist policymakers and community organizers in more effective urban planning practices.
  • Conducted a literature review on middle-class urbanization; Analyzed Suzhou's housing market using ArcGIS and R, visualizing price distribution and changes across the city; Selected key areas (e.g., industrial parks, new districts) based on data analysis; Utilized image recognition from Baidu Street View for further analysis, narrowing down to three specific areas (Shengpu, Yinshan Lake, Taihu New Town); Conducted on-site research in these areas and combined findings with the literature review to assess gentrification.
  • Identified gentrification in Shengpu driven by policy and park development, while gentrification in Yinshan Lake did not align with traditional theories; Highlighted the potential benefits of combining GIS with other analytical tools for future urban development forecasting.

Migratory Dynamics: The Interplay of Environment and Human Factors on White-fronted Geese

  • Studied habitat suitability for migrating Bar-headed Geese in the context of climate change and human impact. Constructed and assessed a Habitat Suitability Model (HSM) to identify critical factors influencing their habitat preferences during migration.
  • Utilized Google Earth Engine to download and classify NDVI and NDWI data; Employed Geodetector for non-linear factor analysis, examining interactions between GPS coordinates and environmental variables.
  • Developed an HSM providing insights into geese habitat preferences amidst changing conditions; Demonstrated proficiency in advanced remote sensing and data analysis techniques; Discovered non-linear relationships, aiding conservation and management strategies.
